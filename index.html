Summary: During the process of unification with the national organization, MKPUSA explicitly represented that the NWTA would remain under the control of local area(s). At the onset of the Covid-19 pandemic (and with presumed good intentions), MKPUSA departed from its agreement and disallowed NWTAs nationwide. Two years later, men who wish to be vaccinated have been; others understand the risks of gathering. For all of the following reasons (see below), it is now time for MKPUSA to facilitate men's work by honoring its stated agreement, returning the NWTA to local control and allowing men and constituent areas to take responsibility for themselves.

“Never doubt that a thoughtful group of committed citizens can change the world, for indeed it is the only thing that ever has.”

If you’ve been around men’s work as represented by the ManKind Project (MKP), you may recognize these sayings as foundational to who we are and what we do.

Consider one more:

“The only thing necessary for the triumph of evil is for good men to do nothing.”

Regarding the NWTA, and in response to Covid (and even with the best of intentions), MKPUSA has arrived at the functional outcome of doing nothing.1 This is your invitation to consider the impact of the current course and what you can do to make a difference. If you care about preserving/​reclaiming the power of the NWTA in a post Covid world, settle in for a detailed discussion.

Two data points inform this effort:

  1. By October 2021, large scale events had begun to return to the Intermountain area, and I attended a concert in a packed arena. The organizers did it right: proof of vaccination or a negative Covid test was required. Because of these requirements, the concert started nearly an hour later than stated, but ticket holders willingly complied with guidelines set forth by local health officials.
  2. One month earlier, Intermountain’s NWTA had been cancelled by MKPUSA in response to “rising infection rates,” even though we had proposed and were planning an NWTA for which vaccinations and other safety measures would be required for all in attendance.

Intermountain had done its homework. An internal survey suggested that greater than 90% of Intermountain men were in favor of a vaccinated weekend. Four medical experts had signed a statement in favor of our plan. Our stated intention for a vaccinated weekend was not about excluding anyone from attending, but to do something safely. In our calculations, an imperfect (but safe) NWTA was better than not holding one at all.

MKPUSA acknowledged receipt of our proposal but never provided a formal reply. In light of this omission, I and several others asked for a Zoom call with an MKPUSA administrator. That call took place on August 17, 2021.

The discussion was a rousing one. Among several topics discussed was one statement that stood out in particular. After discussing the topic of risk with regard to the NWTA, the administrator became tearful and said, “I am not going to have anyone die on my watch.”

My first reaction, then and now, was to honor this man for what I judge(d) to be unstinting loyalty to men’s work in general and to the ManKind Project in particular. I have never questioned this man’s motives.

My second thought was that this man needed support from many others so that the weight of decision making for MKPUSA and for the NWTA didn’t land primarily on him, as it seemed (from his comment) to have done.

My third thought was to reflect that emergencies don’t necessarily alter agreements. With due respect for the motives of MKPUSA (as represented by this administrator), the language under which unification2 was pitched to MKP Intermountain was clear and unambiguous. Unification was represented primarily as an administrative streamlining. Upon unification, decision making for the NWTA was to remain with each local area. From the “Unification Overview” document circulated prior to the vote to unify:

Unification will provide our center with an improved business structure including written guidelines, procedures, roles and responsibilities that will assist us in maintaining solvency and health in perpetuity. To most men in our center, not much will change or look different.

Under the heading, “What will not change” we read:

  • Our Intermountain name, culture, and identity
  • …
  • Our [Intermountain’s] ability to decide when and where we hold NWTAs and other MKP related trainings (emphasis added)
  • Our ability to hold winter retreats, summer gatherings, work weekends, and other local or area-wide non-MKP governed community gatherings and events2

Every NWTA I’ve staffed has clearly differentiated between integrity (acting in accordance with our values) and accountability (acting in accordance with our agreements). Thus even with the best of intentions, MKPUSA can still be out of accountability with specific representations and agreements that have been based on them.

In the ManKind Project, we often teach that to violate agreements is to act out of shadow. We have a defined accountability process by which to identify our shadow(s), acknowledge the impacts of our decisions, and reclaim our power as men in right relationship with others.

The Data

“The world is on fire.” This is nowhere more evident than by the divided approach taken to the Covid pandemic. In some states, masks and vaccines have been mandated. In other states, legislatures have enacted broad protections for organizations, shielding them from liability for Covid exposure, except in cases of “gross negligence, willful misconduct, or failure to follow public health orders…” See “Covid-19 Shield Laws Proliferate Even as Liability Suits Do Not.”

It is likely that any reasonable party can appreciate emergency measures instituted at the beginning of a pandemic. Now, two years later, MKPUSA’s one-size-fits-all policy not only violates its written representations, it makes no allowance for different communities with different sentiments, different laws, different risk profiles, and different cultures. Failure to honor local control of the NWTA is having an escalating impact.

“What’s At Risk?” Every statement I’ve heard regarding MKPUSA’s Covid response has focused on risk to the national organization. The foregone conclusion seems to be that the legal risk of an NWTA outweighs the potential benefits. Research by the Pew Charitable Trusts, one of our nation’s oldest and most respected nonprofit organizations, presents a different view in the article, “States Braced for a Wave of COVID Lawsuits. It Never Arrived.”

Why did the expected wave of lawsuits never arrive? The article explains that, “because of the nature of COVID-19, including how fast and easily it spreads, pinpointing and proving the exact location where someone got the virus is difficult.” In order to be successful, any litigation would have to do just that.

The Impact

For argument’s sake, we will still assume that with respect to Covid, the legal risk of an NWTA is not zero. Here we draw on an NWTA protocol for assistance. Every “What’s At Risk” process on the NWTA consists of two forks in the decision tree. Each man is asked to weigh the risk of moving forward to get what he wants against the risk of staying where he is at. Two years into a pandemic, we can all appreciate some risk. But has MKPUSA properly weighed the risk of holding an NWTA against the risk of continuing to functionally forbid it? What are some of those risks?

  • Suicide. Have you ever heard a man say that the NWTA saved his life? I have. I am one of those men. Can we be Covid “safe” by disallowing the NWTA and still lose more men than we save?
  • Inertia. Is MKPUSA weighing being completely Covid “safe” (e.g., no NWTAs) against the damage done to institutional inertia? The relevance of a nonprofit organization can be measured by the current impact it has on the life of each participant. Inertia built and sustained over many years can be lost comparatively quickly through inaction.
  • Relevance. I have seen firsthand men lose interest in the ManKind Project in favor of organizations that are allowing trainings. Many others (including most Intermountain men) have spoken in favor of separating from MKPUSA because of the concerns outlined above.
  • Financial Problems. Like any organization, MKPUSA requires money to function. By cutting off a primary revenue source in the NWTA, MKPUSA has requested emergency donations. The USLB has insisted on continued payment of dues during a time when leadership development has functionally halted along with the NWTA. These financial problems have resulted from a single, inflexible response to the pandemic. All around us, the world is moving on, learning to function during a pandemic. It’s time for MKPUSA to do so as well.

Cultural Considerations

MKPUSA is currently engaged in the honorable pursuit of becoming more conversant with the richness of cultures and cultural differences. As important as this work is, cultural diversity must mean more than reckoning with the crimes of a dominant culture. At its widest application, diversity reckons not only with misdeeds (or shadow), but also honors the brilliance (or gold) each culture brings to the table. American/Western culture has traditionally celebrated participatory government and representation. MKPUSA has closed working groups that decide matters of policy. By seeking input from the community at large, could MKPUSA obviate the need for this very web site? Is MKPUSA aware of how many within its ranks feel disenfranchised because they have not been accorded a means of voicing their ideas and concerns to MKPUSA administration? Would we do justice to “cultural diversity” by ignoring the strengths inherent in the American cultural contribution of participatory government?

Finally, the ManKind project stresses individual responsibility, teaching each man to ask for what he wants and needs. Two years into a pandemic, those who want vaccinations have gotten them. Others are aware of risks they might take in attending gatherings where close contact is likely. With due respect for the intentions of MKPUSA in ensuring strict Covid safety, further blanket restrictions on the NWTA will be increasingly counterproductive, causing MKPUSA to fail in its relevance, its mission, and its financial viability as an organization.

The Invitation

In light of all of the above, we call on MKPUSA to:

  • Come into accountability with written representations made, during unification, that the NWTA would remain under local area control,
  • Actively facilitate men’s work in general (and the NWTA in particular) instead of standing in its way3
  • Seek representative input from constituent areas and accord them a voice in MKPUSA governance, and
  • Publish MKPUSA’s formulation of Covid risk. If specific legal advice or insurance requirements continue to prevent the NWTA from happening, publish the documents and give men the opportunity to understand why.

Initiation has been called the birthright of men. Many men are willing to take risks to do their work. The time is now.4 The world is (still) on fire, and…

Men are waiting.
